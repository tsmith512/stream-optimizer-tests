Fit Options

Overview

The most common use of the media transformation endpoint is to resize an original asset to match new dimensions. In many cases, the output dimensions will not match the input aspect ratio. Here is a sample of the current behavior of the image transformation endpoint.

Horizontal Input

Square Output

"contain" and "scale-down" scales to maintain aspect ratio, fully contained within the output dimension(s). Thus the processed output may be smaller on one dimenion than provided dimenions. "contain" will allow upscaling where "scale-down" will only reduce size.

"cover" scales to maintain aspect ratio to, fully covering the output dimenisons, then crops the remaining area. Thus the processed output will always match both provided dimensions.

  • "crop" acts like "scale-down" or "cover" depending on the size
  • "pad" acts like "scale-down" then applies a letterbox background
